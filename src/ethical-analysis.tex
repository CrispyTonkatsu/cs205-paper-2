\subsection{Responsibilities of ML companies}
When an ML company decides to produce a model, what are the responsiblities do they have towards all the involved stake-holders? The lack of governance over ML implementations has brought up the need to discuss what are the expectations towards a company's model.

\subsubsection{Kantianism}
Under Kantianism, companies are presented with the following formulations when it comes to a model's bias:

\begin{enumerate}
	\item{If a user damages someone with my product, it was a user's error}
	\item{Lying to others is ethically sound if I don't know better}
	\item{Making money off models that misrepresent people is ethical}
\end{enumerate}

For these formulations it can be seen that there are major contradictions. If everyone could blame their clients for the mistakes that their products make there would be no trust in any industry and therefore no development. For the second formulation runs into a contradiction, where if everyone were allow to spread misinformation, there would be no source of truth for humanity. Lastly, the 3rd formulation is removing humanity out of the minorities in the situation by neglecting the pain that the misrepresentation can cause. Evidence of this pain can be seen in the documentary as people of color are persecuted unfairly which causes them great distress and removes their agency when being judged. (CITE SOURCES HERE)

\subsubsection{Social Contract Theory}
Under social contract theory, instead of evaluating imperatives, there are implicit expectations that we as a society agree upon when integrating a system where machine learning is used:

\begin{enumerate}
	\item{The algorithm will measure biases impartially as it has no previous lived experiences}
	\item{The companies are developing the product with the goal of betterment of society with financial gain as compensation (as is the case with most development under capitalism)}
\end{enumerate}
